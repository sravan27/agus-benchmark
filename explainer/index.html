AGUS

Adaptive Generalization Under Shift

A benchmark measuring whether language models adapt coherently when rules, evidence, representations, or social beliefs shift.

The Diagnostic Gap

Static benchmarks heavily index frozen-task correctness. A model can achieve superlative accuracy on zero-shot benchmarks, yet collapse entirely when underlying facts or rules are perturbed dynamically.

This fails to capture the true architecture of reasoning. AGUS drops the pretense of the frozen prompt, explicitly measuring adaptation, instability, belief revision, and coherence under change in extended multi-turn environments.

AGUS v2

Branching Counterfactual Coherence

Can the model preserve coherent beliefs across minimally changed futures?

AGUS v2 measures invariant preservation. From a shared base state, we evaluate models across nearby alternate futures to measure structural update fidelity without reverting to base-state defaults.

Caveats

  • Results are drawn from three local open-weight models (7B–12B). They demonstrate comparative signal, not universal ranking truth against frontier closed-models.
  • All models remain highly contradiction-blind in absolute terms. E.g., Mistral NeMo 12B exhibits a 0.56 absolute contradiction blindness rate.
